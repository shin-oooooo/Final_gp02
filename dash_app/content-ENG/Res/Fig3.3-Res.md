# Figure3.3 · Two-track Monte Carlo (Research · Category B)

**Object Type (B)**: Numerical model prediction output (risk scenario simulation). This figure maps the optimized combination weight \(w^\*\) of Phase 3 to \(\mu_p,\sigma_p\) at the combination level, and then generates two sets of Monte Carlo paths under the same random source:

- **Base Rail (no jumps)**: Geometric Brownian diffusion term only (continuous small fluctuations).
- **Pressure rail (with jump)**: "Poisson jump" (extreme events) is superimposed on the diffusion term, and the jump intensity/amplitude can be injected by the emotion \(S\) (or \(S_t\) path).

The core narrative of the figure is not "predicted returns", but gives the **tail risk boundary**: using the **risk area** between the "no-jump median track" and the "jump-inclusive 5% quantile track", as well as cloud path comparison, to explain why Level 1/2 defense is necessary and the difference from the Level 0 counterfactual.

This article aligns the main column `dcc.Graph(id="fig-p3-mc")`, which is generated by `dash_app/dashboard_face_render.py` calling `dash_app/figures.py:fig_mc_dual_track(..., figure_title="Figure3.3")`; MC data comes from `snap_json["phase3"]`.

## 3. Model and mathematical objects (implementation consistent)

### 3.1 \(\mu_p,\sigma_p\) at the combination level

Let the weight vector be \(w\), the marginal benefit vector be \(\mu\), and the covariance be \(\Sigma\), then:

\[
\mu_p = w^\top\mu,\qquad \sigma_p=\sqrt{w^\top\Sigma w}.
\]

Implementation: `research/phase3.py:_simulate_mc_paths` (`mu_p`/`sig_p`).

> Engineering lower bound: \(\sigma\) will be clamped to `sig_b = max(sig_p, 0.014)` to avoid near-zero fluctuations causing the path to not move (within the same function).

### 3.2 Euler jump diffusion in logarithmic space (without jump vs with jump)

On a single step \(\Delta t = 1/252\):

\[
\Delta\ln S = \mu_p\Delta t + \sigma\sqrt{\Delta t}\,Z + J\cdot \mathbb{I}\{\text{jump}\},
\]

Among them \(Z\sim\mathcal{N}(0,1)\), the jump event is Bernoulli, and the jump probability within a step is:

\[
p_{\text{step}} = 1-\exp(-\lambda\Delta t).
\]

- **No jump base rail**: Let \(\lambda=0,J=0\).
- **Includes jump pressure rail**: generate jump_inc with \(\lambda,J\) (constant or varies with \(S_t\)) and superimpose to the diffusion increment.

Implementation: `research/phase3.py:jump_diffusion_paths_vectorized` and `annual_jump_intensity_to_step_prob`.

## 4. Semantics of elements in the picture (consistent with the front end)

| Element | Semantics (source code caliber) |
|---|---|
| **Benchmark Cloud** | A collection of hop-free paths drawn by `mc_paths_baseline` (sampling 40, downsampling ≤ 200 points). |
| **Stress Cloud** | A collection of paths with hops drawn by `mc_paths_stress` (sampled 40 + appended "worst final value path out of 10,000"). |
| **Conservative track (no-jump median)** | `mc_path_median_nojump`: Take the median of 10,000 non-jump paths moment by moment, and then downsample. |
| **Pressure rail (including jump P5)** | `mc_path_jump_p5`: Among the 10,000 jump paths, take the "representative path" corresponding to the 5% quantile according to the terminal wealth, and then downsample. |
| **Worst final value path in the cloud** | The path with the smallest terminal among all hop paths, ensuring that the tail extreme is visualized (`worst_idx` points to it). |
| **Risk Area** | The filled area between the "no-jump median rail" and the "jump-containing P5 rail" represents the explainable drop zone from normal to the pressure tail. |

## 5. Key derived indicators (must be reported at the same time when reading the chart)

- `mc_expected_max_drawdown_pct`: Maximum drawdown (%) along **including jump P5 representative path**, as a representative MDD for UI annotation (`research/phase3.py:_simulate_mc_paths`).
- `mc_mdd_p95`: After calculating MDD on **all 10,000 paths including jumps**, take the 95th percentile (%), which represents "tail retracement risk in the sense of path distribution" (same function).

One of the two is "the retracement of a single representative track" and the other is "the tail quantile of the full distribution retracement". They have different purposes and cannot be read interchangeably.

## 9. Relationship with other objects in Phase 3 (Simplified)

- **With Figure3.2 (weights)**: Fig3.2 gives the cross-section of \(w^\*\); Fig3.3 uses the same \(w^\*\) to "unfold the risk along the time axis" into a path distribution and a tail boundary.
- **and defense level**: `defense_level` and `objective_name` determine the weight source (Sharpe / Semantic Penalty / CVaR), thereby changing \(\mu_p,\sigma_p\) and path distribution shape; the jump intensity/amplitude will further amplify the pressure tail with the emotion \(S\).
