## 3. Methodology and mathematical objects

### 3.1 Weight vector and normalization

`fig_p0_portfolio_pie` constructs a vector from the input dictionary `weights`:

- `values[s] = max(weights.get(s, 0), 0)`
- If \sum_s values_s \le 10^{-15}: the entire column is returned to equal weight;
- Otherwise normalize to w_s = values_s / \sum_j values_j.

Therefore the percentages in the graph always satisfy \sum_s w_s = 1 and will never exhibit negative weights.

### 3.2 "Selected sector pull" semantics

When `pie_selected` is present and matches a sector `label`, the sector `pull=0.07` is used to visually lock "the target currently being adjusted by the slider" (see `dash_app/figures.py`).

## 5. Interaction rules (it is recommended to focus on the research model)

### 5.1 "The remaining objects are scaled according to the original proportion" of the slider

Assume that the selected target is s^, and the slider sets the new value w'*{s^}\in[0,1]. The set of remaining targets is S\setminuss^, and the sum of the old weights is O=\sum*{j\ne s^} w_j. Implement scaling using:


w'*j = w_j\cdot\frac{1-w'*{s^}}{O}\quad (j\ne s^)


If O=0, keep the rest as 0 and only update s^ (source code, see `callbacks/p0_assets.py:_pie_slider` about lines 151–163).

### 5.2 Boundaries between drawing and “application”

- The underline only determines "the set of underlying objects currently participating in the combination" and the "equal weight reset rule" (`reset_eq`);
- The pie chart itself is just the visualization and writer of `p0-weight-store`, which **will not** directly trigger the calculation of Phase 0~3; you need to click **Apply/Apply and Recalculate** in the sidebar to enter the pipeline.
