## Purpose and three bundled tests

This chart flags prices/returns that look stationary but have “gone bad,” or regimes where structure is too weak to model—early warning for “broken market logic.” Rolling tests across the window are summarized in three rails:

1. **Pseudo-stationarity**: drift or breaks in mean, variance, tail risk though surface volatility looks tame.
2. **Structural inadequacy**: effective sample size / cross-sectional richness (too few observations, overly uniform correlation, ill-conditioned covariance making estimates unstable).
3. **Consistency / robustness**: whether signals repeat across windows and frequencies with directional persistence (noise rejection).

Persistent activation of any rail raises defense stance and feeds credibility and stress layers with evidence that “structure is no longer reliable.”
